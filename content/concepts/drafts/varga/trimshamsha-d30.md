# Triṃśāṃśa (D-30)

*Triṃśāṃśa* (त्रिंशांश, also written Trimshamsha or Trimsamsa) —
the *D-30* chart — is the thirteenth of the sixteen classical
*Ṣoḍaśa-vargas* described in *Brihat Parāśara Horā Śāstra*. The
Triṃśāṃśa reads the *ariṣṭa* register — classical miseries and
specific afflictions, the register of difficulty-events the native
may classically encounter. The name *triṃśāṃśa* is Sanskrit for
"thirtieth part," reflecting the 30-degree rāśi and signalling that
each part corresponds to one degree — but the D-30 uses a distinctive
*non-uniform* division scheme that sets it apart from all other
supplementary vargas.

## Classical grounding

*Brihat Parāśara Horā Śāstra*'s *Ṣoḍaśa-varga-adhyāya* specifies the
Triṃśāṃśa's distinctive non-uniform computation; *Bṛhat Jātaka* of
Varāhamihira chapter 8 names the same scheme with extended classical
commentary; *Phaladeepikā* and *Saravali* supply extended treatment
of the *ariṣṭa*-register applications. Classical sources uniformly
name the D-30 as the primary varga for specific-affliction
assessment — a register distinct from the general
difficulty-register of the Rāśi's dusthāna-bhāvas.

## Computational scheme

BPHS's division rule for D-30 is non-uniform and differs from every
other classical varga. Sūrya and Chandra are not assigned
triṃśāṃśas; only the five tārā-grahas (Mangala, Śani, Guru, Budha,
Śukra) receive triṃśāṃśa-ownership. In **odd rāśis** (Meṣa, Mithuna,
Siṃha, Tulā, Dhanu, Kumbha), the 30° is divided into five unequal
parts: the first 5° (0°–5°) is Mangala's triṃśāṃśa (Meṣa); the
next 5° (5°–10°) is Śani's (Kumbha); the next 8° (10°–18°) is
Guru's (Dhanu); the next 7° (18°–25°) is Budha's (Mithuna); the
final 5° (25°–30°) is Śukra's (Tulā). In **even rāśis** (Vṛṣabha,
Karka, Kanyā, Vṛścika, Makara, Meena), the division reverses in
graha-order and each graha's rāśi-assignment switches to its
even-rāśi counterpart: the first 5° (0°–5°) is Śukra's (Vṛṣabha);
the next 7° (5°–12°) is Budha's (Kanyā); the next 8° (12°–20°) is
Guru's (Meena); the next 5° (20°–25°) is Śani's (Makara); the
final 5° (25°–30°) is Mangala's (Vṛścika). The degree-ranges
(5-5-8-7-5 and its reverse 5-7-8-5-5) and the graha-rāśi assignments
are fixed by BPHS and must be applied exactly.

## Classical significations

The Triṃśāṃśa-chart classically reads *ariṣṭa* — classical
miseries, specific afflictions, difficulty-events, accident-register
susceptibilities, and the general register of what the classical
tradition names as the native's inherited misfortune-register.
Classical reading: the graha placed in the native's Lagna-
triṃśāṃśa reads strongly for the native's constitutional
difficulty-inclination; malefic placement in the Lagna-triṃśāṃśa
classically names specific difficulty-registers (Mangala-triṃśāṃśa
for injury-register, Śani-triṃśāṃśa for chronic-difficulty register,
and so on per classical graha-register correspondences).

## Classical interpretation register

The classical interpretation register of the Triṃśāṃśa is *ariṣṭa-
specific* — the chart reads a diagnostic register concerning the
difficulty-dimensions of life, not a register that predicts specific
events deterministically. Classical commentators emphasise that the
D-30 supplements the dusthāna-bhāva readings (6/8/12) of the Rāśi-
chart and the Rāśi-chart's malefic placements, and that the D-30
reading is classically a *diagnostic* framework — the tradition
reports the register the chart reads; the reader's life-circumstances
are not determined by the chart. The D-30 is included in classical
graded-varga schemes from *Ṣoḍaśa-varga*. Some classical *ariṣṭa-
bhaṅga* (cancellation-of-affliction) rules specifically reference
the D-30: benefic placement in Lagna-triṃśāṃśa or strong Lagna-
lord presence classically cancels specific afflictions.
