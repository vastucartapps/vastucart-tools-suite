# Navāṃśa (D-9)

*Navāṃśa* (नवांश, also written Navamsa) — the *D-9* chart — is the
sixth of the sixteen classical *Ṣoḍaśa-vargas* described in *Brihat
Parāśara Horā Śāstra* and the most-weighted supplementary varga in
classical reading after the Rāśi itself. The Navāṃśa reads the
*dharma-kalatra* register — spouse and marriage specifically, the
dharmic-fruition dimension of life, and overall fortune — and
supplements the 7th-bhāva and 9th-bhāva readings of the Rāśi-chart.
The name *navāṃśa* is Sanskrit for "ninth part." Classical tradition
regards the D-9 as the primary supplementary varga; *Phaladeepikā*
cites the classical maxim that the Rāśi shows the body and the
Navāṃśa shows the fruit.

## Classical grounding

*Brihat Parāśara Horā Śāstra*'s *Ṣoḍaśa-varga-adhyāya* names the
Navāṃśa's computational rule and its multi-register signification;
*Bṛhat Jātaka* of Varāhamihira chapter 8 treats the D-9 with
particular weight; *Phaladeepikā* chapter 2 devotes extended
classical commentary; *Saravali* and *Jaimini Sūtras* supply
further treatment. Classical sources universally treat the Navāṃśa
as the primary supplementary varga — its weight in classical reading
exceeds that of every other supplementary varga.

## Computational scheme

BPHS's division rule for D-9: each 30° rāśi is divided into nine
3°20' parts (30° ÷ 9 = 3.333°), with rāśi-ownership assigned by
the rāśi's *element* (movable / fixed / dual) as follows — for
movable (*cara*) rāśis (Meṣa, Karka, Tulā, Makara), the navāṃśa-
count begins from the same rāśi; for fixed (*sthira*) rāśis
(Vṛṣabha, Siṃha, Vṛścika, Kumbha), the count begins from the 9th
rāśi from the same; for dual (*dvisvabhāva*) rāśis (Mithuna, Kanyā,
Dhanu, Meena), the count begins from the 5th rāśi from the same.
The count then proceeds through nine consecutive rāśis. Example:
Meṣa's (movable) nine navāṃśas are Meṣa, Vṛṣabha, Mithuna, Karka,
Siṃha, Kanyā, Tulā, Vṛścika, Dhanu. Vṛṣabha's (fixed) nine begin
from Makara and run Makara, Kumbha, Meena, Meṣa, Vṛṣabha, Mithuna,
Karka, Siṃha, Kanyā. Mithuna's (dual) nine begin from Tulā and
run Tulā, Vṛścika, Dhanu, Makara, Kumbha, Meena, Meṣa, Vṛṣabha,
Mithuna. Each 3°20' of navāṃśa-range corresponds to 1 nakshatra-
pāda, so the 108 navāṃśas across the zodiac (12 rāśis × 9
navāṃśas) align with the 108 pādas of the 27 nakshatras (27 × 4).

## Classical significations

The Navāṃśa-chart classically reads: (1) *kalatra* — spouse,
marriage register, marital happiness (the D-9 is the classical
primary spouse-chart); (2) *dharma* — the dharmic-fruition dimension
of the native's life; (3) overall fortune — the classical "fruit
of the chart," read as the mature manifestation of the Rāśi-
chart's potential. Classical reading: the 7th-bhāva of the D-9
reads spouse specifically; the Lagna of the D-9 reads the native's
dharmic-register; the vargottama condition in the D-9 is classically
among the strongest single-graha strength-registers in all of
*jyotiṣa*.

## Classical interpretation register

The classical interpretation register of the Navāṃśa is
*comprehensive-fruit* — the chart reads the maturation of the
Rāśi-chart's significations across life. Classical vargottama
(a graha in the same rāśi in D-1 and D-9) occurs only in specific
3°20' ranges per rāśi-element: the first navāṃśa of movable, the
fifth of fixed, the ninth of dual. This vargottama-D-9 is classically
the single most significant strength-register in *Ṣoḍaśa-varga*
analysis. The D-9 is included in every graded-varga scheme from
*Ṣaḍ-varga* onward, with the strictest *Ṣoḍaśa-varga* including it.
Graha *debilitation-cancellation* (Nīca-bhaṅga) and *exaltation-
confirmation* are classically read strongest when reinforced by D-9
placement — a Rāśi-debilitated graha exalted in D-9 carries a
classical strength-reversal reading.
