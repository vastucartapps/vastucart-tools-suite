# Śaśa-yoga

*Śaśa-yoga* (शशयोग, also written Sasa Yoga or Shasha Yoga) is the
fifth and final of the five *Pañca-Mahāpuruṣa-yogas*, produced when
*Śani* is placed in its own rāśi (*Makara* or *Kumbha*) or
exaltation rāśi (*Tulā*) in a *kendra* (1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th
bhāva) from the Lagna. The yoga name *śaśa* — classically glossed
as "hare" in the literal Sanskrit register and as an alternate
name for the moon-spot animal in classical iconography — carries
in the yoga-phala context the register of enduring presence and
patient accumulation of authority. Śani's classical significations
(discipline, endurance, long-duration work, authority through tested
commitment, karmic responsibility) are activated in the *mahāpuruṣa*
register when the graha occupies classical strength in a kendra.

## Classical grounding

*Phaladeepikā* 6.5 is the canonical verse for Śaśa-yoga; *Bṛhat
Parāśara Horā Śāstra* treats it in its yoga-phala chapters;
*Saravali* chapter 35 and *Bṛhat Jātaka* of Varāhamihira provide
extended treatment. Classical commentators name Śaśa's register as
endurance-authoritative — distinguished capacity in long-duration
leadership, administration over sustained periods, institutional
authority earned through demonstrated competence, and the kind of
prominence that arrives late but holds. Classical literary biography
often cites Śaśa-yoga in accounts of long-reigning administrators,
senior judges, and leaders whose contribution measures across
decades rather than years.

## Formation rules

Primary classical form: Śani in Makara, Kumbha, or Tulā, placed in
the 1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th bhāva from Lagna. Classical strongest
Śaśa: Śani in Tulā (exaltation; exaltation degree at 20° Tulā) in
a kendra — the combined *uccha-bala* and *kendra-bala* produce the
maximum classical configuration. Śani in Kumbha (*mūla-trikoṇa*
for Śani: 0°–20° of Kumbha) in the 7th bhāva (where Śani's
*digbala* operates) is classically named as particularly strong.
Some classical sources allow the kendra to be counted from Chandra
as well as from Lagna.

## Classical manifestation pattern

The Śaśa register classically inclines the native toward the
endurance-authoritative domain — long-duration leadership,
administration, institutional governance, work in domains where
outcomes accumulate across years or decades rather than through
sprint-like achievement. Classical reading names distinguished
patience, capacity to bear responsibility over sustained periods,
classical "old-age-rising" trajectory where prominence arrives
later in life and holds thereafter, and authority earned through
demonstrated discipline. The domains of expression classically
named include senior administrative and judicial roles, long-term
engineering and construction, political and institutional leadership
requiring endurance, service in ascetic or renunciant traditions,
work with marginalised or labour registers, and any field where the
Śani-signification of tapas (disciplined effort over time) is the
professional core.

## Strength modulation

Classical strength rules: (1) exaltation in Tulā is strongest; (2)
7th bhāva placement is classically strongest kendra for Śaśa given
Śani's digbala in the 7th; (3) retrograde Śani is common (Śani is
retrograde approximately 140 days per year) — classical reading
varies, some sources strengthen the introspective register, others
modify; (4) aspect of Guru moderates — Guru's aspect on Śani
classically softens the register toward dharmic authority, and the
combination is classically considered favourable despite the
Guru-Śani naisargika enmity at the graha level; (5) aspect of
Mangala complicates — the combination classically carries
contest-inflected weight that modifies the patience register; (6)
aspect of Sūrya complicates further — Sūrya-Śani enmity affects
the register when the aspect is close; (7) aspect of Śukra
strengthens — both are classical friends; (8) combustion is rare
given Śani's orbital distance.

category), the 7th and 10th Bhāva pages (primary manifestation
