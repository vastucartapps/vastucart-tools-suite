# Chaldean Numerology

*Chaldean Numerology* is one of the two major Western numerological
systems (the other being Pythagorean). Its central feature is a
distinctive letter-to-number table assigning values 1–8 to the Latin
alphabet — 9 is reserved and not assigned to any letter. The system
is attributed by later Western occult tradition to ancient Chaldean
(Mesopotamian) sources, popularised in the English-speaking world
by Cheiro (Count Louis Hamon, 1866–1936) and his successors. Most
modern scholars read Chaldean Numerology as a Western-occult-tradition
synthesis with ancient naming but substantially modern
systematisation; the specific letter-value table as used today is
not well-attested in surviving ancient Mesopotamian texts.

## Tradition and grounding

Cheiro's published writings introduced Chaldean Numerology to the
broad English-speaking audience; Sepharial and later Western
practitioners extended the tradition. The distinctive Chaldean
letter-table assigns values based on claimed-sound-vibration rather
than positional alphabetical order. In Indian Vedic numerology, the
Chaldean letter-table is adopted for *nāmāṅka* (name number)
computation — making Chaldean letter-values the operative convention
for Indian name-numerology, even though the interpretive framework
applied is Indian Vedic graha-correspondence.

## Computation method

**Chaldean letter-to-number table:**

A, I, J, Q, Y = 1  
B, K, R = 2  
C, G, L, S = 3  
D, M, T = 4  
E, H, N, X = 5  
U, V, W = 6  
O, Z = 7  
F, P = 8  

No letter is assigned value 9 — the number 9 is classically reserved
in Chaldean tradition for divine or sacred register and is held
outside the letter-value assignments.

**Name-number computation:** assign each letter in the native's
full name its Chaldean value, sum all values, reduce by digit-sum
until a single-digit 1–8 or the number 9 (which can appear in
reduction results) is reached. Unlike Western Pythagorean master-
number retention, Chaldean tradition does not emphasise master-
number retention at 11, 22, or 33 as a primary interpretive feature.

## Tradition-specific interpretation register

Chaldean Numerology reads the computed single-digit as indicating
occult-tradition registers of character, potential, and name-
influenced destiny. The framework emphasises *sound vibration* of
the name more strongly than Pythagorean tradition — treating
phonetic character as more interpretively significant than visual
spelling, a perspective derived from the tradition's Semitic-
linguistic roots.

## How Chaldean Numerology differs from Indian Vedic Numerology

Although Indian Vedic numerology typically uses the Chaldean letter-
table for *nāmāṅka* computation, the two traditions diverge
interpretively in three important ways. First, the Chaldean tradition
reads the name-number through its own occult-symbolic framework;
Indian Vedic numerology reads the same computed digit through the
graha-correspondence framework (1 = Sūrya, 2 = Chandra, and so on),
connecting it to the classical Jyotiṣa graha-significations. Second,
Chaldean Numerology does not classically compute a separate *mūlāṅka*
or *bhāgyāṅka* from the birth date — the Indian framework's date-
derived number-pair is not part of the Chaldean system. Third, the
number 9 holds fundamentally different registers in the two
traditions: Chaldean holds 9 as divine/sacred reserved number;
Indian Vedic numerology assigns 9 to Mangala and treats it as one
of the nine graha-correspondence numbers like any other. VastuCart's
ecosystem uses Indian Vedic numerology as its primary tradition for
consistency with the Jyotiṣa corpus; the Chaldean letter-table is
adopted as the operative name-letter-value system because it is the
convention modern Indian Vedic numerology practitioners have
consolidated around.

## Tradition-reported practices

Chaldean Numerology practitioners suggest name-spelling adjustments
to align the name-number with classically favourable register. Indian
Vedic practitioners using the Chaldean letter-table for *nāmāṅka*
interpret the same practice through graha-correspondence. The
Chaldean tradition's emphasis on phonetic-register over visual
spelling means digital calculators reading letters from text input
may not capture the nuance the tradition's sound-register reading
intends. These are tradition-reported practices; the reader's
decisions are the reader's.
