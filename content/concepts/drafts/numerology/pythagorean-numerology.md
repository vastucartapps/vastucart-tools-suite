# Pythagorean Numerology

*Pythagorean Numerology* is the most widely-used numerological system
in contemporary Western practice. It takes its name from Pythagoras,
the 6th-century BCE Greek philosopher whose school (the Pythagorean
brotherhood at Croton) developed mathematical-mystical doctrines
treating number as the foundation of cosmic order. The specific
letter-to-number table used in contemporary Pythagorean Numerology
— assigning A=1, B=2, and cycling 1 through 9 across the Latin
alphabet — is substantially modern systematisation. Historical
Pythagorean fragments do not preserve this exact table; the modern
form consolidated in the late 19th and 20th centuries through
Western occult and numerological writers.

## Tradition and grounding

The Pythagorean brotherhood's classical teachings survive primarily
through later sources — Philolaus, Plato's *Timaeus*, and
Neoplatonist commentators. Classical teachings emphasised the
interpretive significance of numbers 1 through 10, with 10 as the
sacred *tetractys*. The modern letter-to-number assignment used in
contemporary Pythagorean Numerology was consolidated by Western
numerological writers in the late 19th century, including L. Dow
Balliett and her successors. The contemporary tradition is
Pythagorean-*descended* rather than a direct preservation — the
interpretive framework is 20th-century Western synthesis, not
classical Greek.

## Computation method

**Pythagorean letter-to-number table:**

1: A, J, S  
2: B, K, T  
3: C, L, U  
4: D, M, V  
5: E, N, W  
6: F, O, X  
7: G, P, Y  
8: H, Q, Z  
9: I, R  

The table assigns values 1–9 cyclically across the alphabet in
positional order (A=1, B=2, ..., I=9, J=1 again, and so on). Unlike
the Chaldean table, all nine single-digit values are used.

**Life Path computation:** sum all digits of the full birth date
(DD + MM + YYYY), reduce by digit-sum until a single digit 1–9
remains, except that 11, 22, and 33 are retained as master numbers
per the standard modern convention.

**Name-letter computations** (Expression, Soul Urge, Personality,
etc.): assign Pythagorean values to the relevant letter subset,
sum, and reduce with master-number retention as applicable.

## Tradition-specific interpretation register

Contemporary Pythagorean Numerology reads the nine single-digit
numbers through archetypal meanings: 1 — leadership; 2 —
partnership; 3 — creative expression; 4 — structure; 5 — freedom;
6 — nurture; 7 — introspection; 8 — material mastery; 9 —
completion. Master Numbers 11, 22, 33 carry heightened registers
of 2, 4, 6 respectively. These archetypal associations are modern
Western-tradition formulations, not documented in surviving
Pythagorean primary sources.

## How Pythagorean Numerology differs from Indian Vedic Numerology

Pythagorean Numerology differs from Indian Vedic numerology in four
primary respects. First, the letter-value table: Pythagorean uses
A=1, B=2 cycling through 1–9; Indian Vedic numerology (for *nāmāṅka*
computation) uses the Chaldean letter-value table. Second, master-
number retention: Pythagorean retains 11, 22, 33 as master numbers;
Indian Vedic numerology reduces these to 2, 4, 6 by standard
recursion. Third, interpretive framework: Pythagorean reads numbers
through Western archetypal meanings; Indian Vedic numerology reads
the same numbers through graha-correspondence (1 = Sūrya, 2 =
Chandra, and so on through 9 = Mangala) and classical Jyotiṣa
significations. Fourth, date-derived register: Indian Vedic
numerology distinguishes *mūlāṅka* (day-only) from *bhāgyāṅka*
(full-date); Pythagorean combines these into the single Life Path
Number. VastuCart's ecosystem uses Indian Vedic as primary for
Jyotiṣa-corpus coherence; Pythagorean is covered for readers
exploring Western numerology.

## Tradition-reported practices

Pythagorean Numerology practitioners name specific colours, days,
and practices corresponding to the archetypal number-meanings.
Name-spelling adjustments, business-name numerology, and date-
selection for major events are common tradition-reported practices.
These are Western-tradition reports; this page does not prescribe
them. Pythagorean and Chaldean are *both* Western occult-tradition
descents — the two yield different readings for the same name.
